Treaty 6 today

and how it still holds up



Though it is a legal agreement today, Treaty 6 is seen by many First Nations as a promise that has not been fully kept. Signed in 1876, it included commitments like land, healthcare, education, and support during hard times. While the government follows the written version, Indigenous communities often focus on the oral agreements and the spirit of partnership. Because of this difference, issues like poor healthcare, underfunded schools, and land conflicts still exist. Treaty 6 remains important, but it also reminds many people of the need for fairness, respect, and stronger relationships between First Nations and the government.
